About me

Hi! My name is Ellena and I am a senior computer science student at the University of Vienna. I am passionate about machine learning and its applications to solve scientific problems. Currently, I am particularly interested in research at the intersection of machine learning and biology.

I am currently a staff tutor (TA) at the Research Group Theory and Applications of Algorithms for the two courses Mathematical Foundations of Computer Science 1 and Numerical Algorithms.

I have previously worked as a student research assistant at the Research Group Theory and Applications of Algorithms, where I focused on developing machine learning solutions to problems in high-performance computing (HPC). I have also interned at the Research Group Distributed AI Systems (R&D) at Siemens Austria, where I worked on implementing a multimodal deep learning model as an imitation learning solution for the automation of a manufacturing process.

In my free time, I enjoy taking dance classes, figure skating and playing board games with friends and family.
